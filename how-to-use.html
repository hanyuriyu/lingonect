How-to-use Guide

A quick guide to engines, methodologies, and formality.

First and foremost, Lingonect is not a volume translation tool!

It is for words, phrases, sentences, and short paragraphs. Avoid excessive or consecutive copying and pasting of text, as this may cause the system to stop working.

NMTs vs. LLMs

When using Lingonect, it is important to understand the difference between NMTs (Neural Machine Translation) and LLMs (Large Language Models). While all engines support translation, more complex methodologies — such as localization, transcreation, culturalization, and analysis — are supported only by LLMs. Please note that if you select these methodologies for an NMT engine, you will not receive a response.

NMTs always use bilingual corpora and are designed to translate by following the source text as closely as possible. While translation remains subjective, particularly in longer or more complex content pieces, translation allows for less creative interpretation than the other three methodologies.

Translation

Sticks as close as possible to the source. In some cases it has to select an entirely different expression or idiom, as it does not exist in the target, but otherwise it must be a direct reflection of the source text.

Localization

Localization involves adjusting translated content to fit specific formats, platforms, or media requirements. For example, when translating Google Ads copy from English to German, character limitations may require you to condense the text, as German often requires more space than English. Similarly, website localization may require adapting measurements, currencies, and formats to suit the target culture.

On Lingonect, the localization methodology provides a translation that is automatically adjusted to your desired length. Simply enter the maximum character count for your translation, and the tool will localize the text to fit those constraints.

After selecting localization with a character max limit, you can select translation, transcreation, or culturalization. Localization cannot be combined with analysis. If you select only localization, the system will automatically use translation as the primary methodology.

Transcreation

Transcreation captures the intent, style, and meaning of the source, then recreates it in the target. It is possible that the target text is quite different from the source, so that it best reflects the source intent.

Culturalization

Culturalisation starts from the target culture, not the source. It reimagines the text to fit the linguistic and cultural reality of the target market, drawing on its references, idioms, sensitivities, and conventions so the message belongs there rather than being adapted into it.

Analyse

Analysis does not translate. Instead it takes your source word, phrase, or sentence and explains its syntax, grammar, style, and meaning — written in the target language you have selected, so a learner can understand the source text. Because it produces an explanation rather than a translation, localization cannot be combined with analysis and the formality level has no effect.

Selecting a methodology

When selected, the chosen methodology icon will turn bright green. If localization is selected and then another methodology is added, localization will turn a darker shade of green.

Formality levels

You can also select a formality level. Some formality levels are only relevant to certain languages:

Honorific and humble is only relevant to languages like Japanese, Korean, Thai, and Vietnamese. Original means that the target text reflects, as closely as possible, the source text's formality level. Ceremonial is reserved for very formal, official occasions. The rest of the formality levels should be self-explanatory.
